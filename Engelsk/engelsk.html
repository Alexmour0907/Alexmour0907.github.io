Singapore: The garden city

Singapore is a vibrant island country located in southeast Asia, well known for its innovative technology and lush natural beauty. Singapore can often be referred to as the “Garden city,” Singapore is famous for its green spaces and, and its commitment to sustainable urban development and living. Singapore uses the lion head as its national symbol. It represents Strength, excellence and courage.

Singapore Zoo

The Singapore zoo is a perfect attraction especially for families when visiting Singapore.
The zoo is so unique because of its big variation of animals from all over the world!
It has over 4,200 animals and about 300 species. Singapore zoo is also very famous
for its “open concept” exhibits, where the goal is to let the animals move around as
freely as possible making it more naturalistic.


When visiting the zoo, you will be able to see rare animals like white rhino, African lions and the sun bear.
There are also free to watch animal shows and a chance to feed some of the animals yourself.

for more information on ticket prices and more visit the zoo's website here.

Marina Bays & Gardens by the Bay

Marina Bays is one of ten distinct districts of Singapore where you can see fine examples of modern architecture. This is a place to discover the modern and trendy part of Singapore.

Whitin Marina Bays there is a place called Marina Bay Sands. It’s famous for its luxurious and top-notch hotel and infinity pool rooftop. It’s also a leading leisure and entertainment destination in the whole of Asia. Marina Bay Sands also has a more affordable Art Science Museum. With tickets costing 53$ to enter the museum.

Another astonishing part of the Marina bays is the “Gardens by the bay” park. A beautiful park area really showing why
Singapore can be regarded as the garden city. It often has special events and carnivals throughout the year.
There are many different sections with different prices. For more information you can check out their main website here.

Singapore national museum & Memorials

The national Museum of Singapore is a great place to visit if you want to learn more about the country.
The museum is the oldest in the country and was built in the 19th century and offers many
perspectives on the history and culture of Singapore.
A day entrance fee for foreign tourists is 10$ and its free access for kids.


A great place to visit would be the Kranji War memorial. It is one of the most popular
historical places in Singapore.
The memorial honors the ones who were killed during the second world war.

The Kranji war memorial has over 4,400 gravestones and additionally
has the graves of 69 Chinese soldiers who died during the Japanese occupation of Singapore.
The memorial however is not only exclusive for soldiers, it also has the graves of the first two presidents of Singapore.
