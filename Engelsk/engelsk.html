Singapore: The garden city

Singapore is a vibrant island country located in southeast Asia, well known for its innovative technology and lush natural beauty. Singapore can often be referred to as the “Garden city,” Singapore is famous for its green spaces and, and its commitment to sustainable urban development and living. Singapore uses the lion head as its national symbol. It represents Strength, excellence and courage.


Practical information


This information may be useful when visiting Singapore.


Weather and time zone

Singapore's tropical climate (25-33C) gives you the opportunity to enjoy summer all year long. Dress, accordingly, choose light clothing but be aware that showers might occur - bring an umbrella. Singapore uses the GMT +8 time zone.

Language

Singapore does have four official languages, and one of them is English. You should be perfectly fine with English, but you may also hear people talk mandarin.


Visa

Even though most tourists do not require Visa, it is recommended to doble check the latest requirements. To ensure you don’t get a troubled start to your trip.

Singapore visitor Centers


If you need any assistance or have any questions, you can swing by any of the visitor centers.
You will be able to get tips on recommended visiting points across the entire Country.


Particularly strict environmental regulations


Singapore takes its environmental laws seriously. Littering is more than only frowned-upon, it can lead to hefty fines or even jail time.
So, remember to keep an eye out for trash bins and make sure to dispose of your waste properly.


Singapore Zoo


The Singapore zoo is a perfect attraction especially for families when visiting Singapore.
The zoo is so unique because of its big variation of animals from all over the world!
It has over 4,200 animals and about 300 species. Singapore zoo is also very famous
for its “open concept” exhibits, where the goal is to let the animals move around as
freely as possible making it more naturalistic.


When visiting the zoo, you will be able to see rare animals like white rhino, African lions and the sun bear.
There are also free to watch animal presentations and a chance to feed the animals yourself.
